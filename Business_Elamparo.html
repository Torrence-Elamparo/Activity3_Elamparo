About Filipino Snacks and Confectionaries


Open Since 1965

The Filipino Christmas Sweets: Polvoron, Yema, and Pastillas Christmas in the Philippines isn't just about decorations and carols; it's a symphony of flavors, especially when it comes to sweets! Polvoron, Yema, and Pastillas are not just treats; they're a reflection of the sweet traditions that define Filipino Christmas.

What is special about Filipino Snacks? Filipino desserts are a confectionery smorgasbord that should not be missed on your trip to the Philippines! Like most of the food you can find in the country, many of the desserts come from a variety of origins and cultures, and this creates a unique experience of flavors.

Our Menu


Pastillas

Pastillas de leche is a favorite sweet delicacy among Filipinos and is traditionally made from buffalo's milk and sugar. It is soft, with smooth texture and creamy rich taste.


Polvoron

Polvoron is a Filipino-style shortbread made of toasted flour, powdered milk, sugar, and butter. Sweet, buttery, and easy to customize with different flavors, it's a delicious snack or dessert and great for gift-giving, too!


Yema

Yema is a sweet custard confectionery from the Philippines. It is made with egg yolks, milk, and sugar.


Crinkles

Chocolate Crinkles are a deliciously soft, fudge-like cookie that are encased in a generous coating of confectioners (powdered or icing) sugar.


Sans Rival

Sans rival is a Filipino dessert cake made of layers of buttercream, meringue and chopped cashews. Its name means "unrivaled" in French. The cake may be decorated, left plain or garnished with pistachios.
